🤗
1 Bodies & Space
Keep hands/feet/objects to yourself. Don't hit, push, or hurt anyone. "Stop" means stop immediately. Respect others' bubble - if someone moves away, don't follow.

Example: Touching after "Stop"? → Redo (hands in pockets, move 2 steps back) → If repeated: separate space for 15 min and 10 repair minutes helping the sibling (or do their quick chore).
👂
2 Attention
Pay attention when you are spoken to and respond to show respect. If you need time to respond, say so—that is OK too. But remember that Dad has the responsibility to keep us going, so he has to have the authority and opportunity to do so, and that starts with listening and responding.

Example: No "Got it" or plan ask? → Redo the response → If repeated: screen pause per ladder until the first step is started.
🏠
3 Stewardship
Take care of what is entrusted to you—yourself, above all, and things that are given to you. Try to only put things back where they belong, but leave things better than you found them. If you see something that should be thrown away, even if you didn't leave it there, throw it away. We are a team, a family.

Example: Gear left out? → Redo → Holding Shelf until tomorrow or earn-back with a 2-minute tidy.
📚
4 First Things First
Everyone wants to do the fun things first. But the first things come first. The first things are our responsibilities. Morning/Evening checklists, homework, chores. When these things are done, then it's time for fun.

Example: Screens before checklist? → Screen pauses; do checklist; resume when done. Chronic tardy mornings: you "pay back" lost dad time with one quick help-job after school.
💝
5 Honesty is the Only Policy
Tell the truth. If you break/forget/hurt: own it → apologize → repair it. "God can save the sinner you are, not the saint you pretend to be."

Example: Admit damage right away: help fix/replace; praise honesty. If hidden: same repair + extra repair minutes.
💭
6 Think of Others First
Sometimes we can hurt each other when we never even imagined that could be possible. So we need to ask ourselves how what we are going to say or do is going to make someone else feel (not just how it might make us feel).

Example: Rude tone/name-calling? → Redo the sentence respectfully → If repeated: conversation pause (10 min) + apology/repair (kind note or act).
📱
7 Screen Time
God did not create you to just look at a screen but also to look at the world and other people. Don't just consume what other people are doing, but do things yourselves so you can figure out what unique talents God has given you and what will give you purpose and happiness.

Example: Device in off-limits place or time? → Park device for remainder of the hour (1st) → rest of afternoon/evening (2nd) → rest of day (3rd).
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
8 We Are More Than a Team
We are a Family. Don't always worry if someone else's task is more difficult than yours, or if someone else got something you didn't. Comparison is the thief of joy. And especially when we are all in the same boat. Sibling Peace Code: If used successfully → bonus praise/token. If they escalate: both kids to calm space → redo Peace Code → repair.
🛡️
9 Obedience
Dad may not always be right, and you will have a chance to reason with him. But sometimes, you will have to accept his decision. This is important and a commandment from God. It is not arbitrary but by design. Dad has been around longer, and plus you have to have someone to decide—think of the example of Buridan's Donkey. We need a final authority.

Example: Misuse of tools/bikes/kitchen? → Immediate pause for the day; review safety; earn-back with supervised use + repair if any damage.

🛡️ Children's Rights (What You Can Expect from Dad)

1. Calm voice & respect. Dad uses a calm voice—no yelling, mocking, or shaming. If he's heated, he calls a 60-second Pause and resets.
2. Clear expectations. Dad shows what "done" looks like in one sentence or with a picture.
3. Chance to make it right. Before any consequence, there's a Repair Window: 5 minutes on a visible timer to fix the issue.
4. Daily 1:1 time (15 min each). Antoine, Cassia, and Julian each get 15 minutes of distraction-free Special Time. Missed time is made up within 48 hours.
5. Opinions are heard. Dad listens fully, repeats back what he heard, considers it, and explains the decision (even when it's "no").
6. One appeal. You may make one respectful appeal per decision with new info; Dad will reconsider once, quickly.
7. Focused attention. When you need help, Dad gives a 5-minute Focus Window—no phone or computer—to start/finish a task.
8. Explanations, not just orders. For non-urgent things, Dad explains the "why." For urgent/safety: follow now, explain right after.
9. Real apology & repair. Dad uses the 3 R's—Recognize, Repair, Restore (and models the redo).
10. Same cleanliness rules. Dad follows the same reset rules as everyone.

🌱 How We Learn & Grow

1. Gentle Reminder & Redo It Right
Quick reset - chance to do it right (0-10 seconds). Let's try that again the right way! Make right whatever you did wrong (fix what was affected, clean, replace, apology note, offer 10 helper minutes).
2. Pause & Think
Take a break from the activity to think about better choices.
3. Take a Break from Other Things
Take a break from other things going on to give you even more time to figure out how to address what is going on (screens, extra curriculars).
4. Fresh Start
Quick plan together, then clean slate! We'll work together to make a plan for moving forward.
