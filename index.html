Preamble

We, the Nahas family, in order to form a more loving and supportive home, establish fairness, ensure peace in our house, provide for our common growth, secure the blessings of connection, and above all to grow close to Jesus Christ, our Lord and Savior, do establish this Constitution for our family. This Constitution is an act of stewardship—caring for the precious gift God has given us—built on the principles of repair, fairness, and growth.

Part I: Our Family Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To create a Christian home, centered in the Orthodox faith, where we are formed in virtue and grow closer to Jesus Christ. We rely on God's grace in all our interactions.

Our family's guiding purpose is to create a Christian environment, grounded in the faith maintained by the Body of Christ, the One Holy Catholic and Apostolic Church, the Orthodox Church. This environment is where we are formed in virtue, as we discern the Holy Spirit's specific goals for each of us and are conformed to the likeness of Christ.

"As for me and my house, we will serve the Lord." - Joshua 24:15
"Behold, children are a heritage from the Lord, The fruit of the womb is a reward." - Psalm 127:3
"You shall teach them diligently to your children, and shall talk of them when you sit in your house, when you walk by the way, when you lie down, and when you rise up." - Deuteronomy 6:7

Part II: Our Core Virtues

The Lord has not created us to guarantee an easy or pleasurable life, or to ensure that all of our desires are always met, though sometimes He will see fit to bestow these things upon us. He has created us to turn us into something awesome, something incredible, eternal, something that reflects Him. This process of forming us may often be hard. We are created to participate in the very life of God and draw upon divine energy to achieve virtues that will fill our lives with purpose and peace.

"And above all these virtues put on love, which binds them all together in perfect unity." - Colossians 3:14

The Three Theological Virtues (The Foundational Virtues - Gifts from God)

✝ Faith

To believe in God, trust in Him, commit ourselves entirely to Him, and participate in the life of the Church, His Body. Trusting God completely and participating in the life of the Church.

⚓ Hope

To desire the Lord and the kingdom of heaven as our true happiness, trusting not in our own strength but in Christ's promises and the Holy Spirit.

❤️ Love

The highest virtue, by which we love God above all things and our neighbor as ourselves. Love binds all other virtues together.

The Fruits of the Spirit

"But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control." - Galatians 5:22-23

💕 Love

To love one another with mutual affection. Loving one another with mutual affection.

😊 Joy

To find delight in God and His blessings, even in difficult times. Finding delight in God, even in difficult times.

🕊️ Peace

To live in harmony, trusting in God who is the source of true peace. Living in harmony, trusting in God.

⏳ Patience

To persevere through challenges without frustration. Persevering through challenges without frustration.

🤲 Kindness

To treat others with warmth and a desire to meet their real needs. Treating others with warmth and meeting their needs.

✨ Goodness

To desire and work for the blessing of our neighbor. Desiring and working for the blessing of others.

🛡️ Faithfulness

To be steadfast in our commitments to God and to one another. Being steadfast in our commitments.

🌿 Gentleness

To have "gentle strength," moderating our anger with a humble spirit. "Gentle strength," moderating anger with humility.

💪 Self-Control

To have rule over our own spirit, especially when we feel anger rising. Having rule over our own spirit, especially anger.

Other Daily Virtues

🤝 Forgiveness

To release resentment and choose to pardon others when they wrong us, just as Christ forgave us.

💬 Honesty

To speak truthfully and act with integrity, avoiding deception in word and deed.

🙏 Humility

To have a modest view of our own importance, recognizing our dependence on God and others.

📋 Responsibility

To be accountable for our duties and commitments, completing what we promise to do.

🙏 Gratitude

To recognize and appreciate God's blessings and the kindness others show us.

⚡ Diligence

To work hard and persist in our efforts, giving our best to every task before us.

These foundational ideas govern our home, flowing from our core virtues.

🤝
1. Kindness, Respect & Unity

🏠
2. Responsibility & Stewardship

Part IV: Our Operational Rules - FAMILY FIRST

This is the code we live by. These rules are the practical application of our Guiding Principles.

👂
F - Follow Instructions

Listen immediately when spoken to. Make eye contact and acknowledge. Obey first, discuss later when calm. Do not interrupt.

Virtues Cultivated: Humility (accepting guidance), Faithfulness (keeping commitments to obey), Self-Control (managing impulses to interrupt or argue)

Scripture: "Children, obey your parents in the Lord, for this is right." - Ephesians 6:1

Consequence Ladder: Gentle Redo → Pause & Think → Screen privilege pause
✔️
A - Act Responsibly

KEYSTONE RULE: Everything has a home. Clean up your own messes immediately. Complete daily responsibilities without reminders. Complete tasks without reminders.

Virtues Cultivated: Responsibility (being accountable for duties), Diligence (working hard without being told), Stewardship (caring for our home and belongings)

Scripture: "Let all things be done decently and in order." - 1 Corinthians 14:40

Consequence Ladder: Responsible for cleanup → Screen pause if ignored → Earn back with complete reset
📱
M - Mind Your Screen

No screens during weekdays except in exceptional circumstances. Weekend baseline: 2 hours after chores. Create more than consume. Dock by 8:30 PM.

Virtues Cultivated: Self-Control (moderating desires for entertainment), Diligence (prioritizing responsibilities first), Wisdom (choosing what is beneficial over what is merely permissible)

Scripture: "All things are lawful for me, but not all things are helpful." - 1 Corinthians 6:12

Consequence Ladder: Immediate pause for misuse → Earn back with creative/outdoor project
💬
I - Interact Kindly (The Peace Code)

Use the Peace Code: "I feel..." (emotion), "When you..." (action), "Please..." (request). Think before speaking. Forgive quickly.

Virtues Cultivated: Kindness (treating others with warmth), Gentleness (using gentle strength in conflict), Patience (persevering without frustration), Love (seeking what's best for others)

Scripture: "Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another." - Ephesians 4:32

Consequence Ladder: Calm Space → Kind Ladder → Privilege pause
🤚
L - Love Your Space

Keep hands, feet, and objects to yourself. "Stop" means freeze immediately. Respect personal bubbles.

Virtues Cultivated: Self-Control (managing impulses to touch or intrude), Gentleness (using careful strength with our bodies), Love (respecting others' physical boundaries)

Scripture: "Do you not know that your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit?" - 1 Corinthians 6:19

Consequence Ladder: Gentle Redo → Pause & Think → Loss of group activities until repair
⚖️
Y - Yield to Truth

Always tell the truth, even when difficult. Own mistakes immediately. Honest confession meets grace. Confession brings grace.

Virtues Cultivated: Honesty (speaking truthfully even when difficult), Humility (admitting mistakes without defensiveness), Courage (facing consequences rather than hiding)

Scripture: "Therefore, putting away lying, let each one of you speak truth." - Ephesians 4:25

Consequence: Truth leads to grace and simple repair. Lies lead to loss of privileges and formal restoration.
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
F - Family Unity

No comparison of chores, privileges, or belongings. Celebrate each other's successes. Pray together daily. Do not compare. Help without keeping score.

Virtues Cultivated: Love (seeking others' good over our own advantage), Peace (living in harmony without rivalry), Faithfulness (staying loyal to family bonds)

Scripture: "Behold, how good and how pleasant it is for brethren to dwell together in unity!" - Psalm 133:1

Consequence Ladder: Gentle Redo for complaining → Family Huddle for patterns
🌟
I - Inspire Joy

Say "thank you" for all actions. Focus on blessings, not lacks. Share gratitude during nightly prayers. Focus on blessings, not what we lack.

Virtues Cultivated: Joy (finding delight in God's blessings), Gratitude (recognizing and appreciating what we have), Goodness (working for others' blessing and happiness)

Scripture: "In everything give thanks." - 1 Thessalonians 5:18

Consequence Ladder: Joy Reset (name 5 grateful things) → Privilege pause for persistent negativity
🎯
R - Respect All

No yelling or screaming. Use inside voices. Walk to speak, don't shout. Count to 10 when angry. When angry, take a "Pause & Think."

Virtues Cultivated: Gentleness (using gentle strength even when upset), Self-Control (managing anger and volume), Patience (being slow to anger and quick to listen)

Scripture: "Let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath." - James 1:19

Consequence Ladder: Volume Check → Calm Space → Privilege pause for outbursts
🎨
S - Seek Purpose

Try new things and learn from mistakes. Use screen time for learning and discovery. Share what you learn. Be willing to try new things and learn from mistakes.

Virtues Cultivated: Diligence (working hard to develop our gifts), Humility (accepting that mistakes are part of learning), Faithfulness (being steadfast in pursuing God's purpose for us)

Scripture: "For we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works." - Ephesians 2:10

Consequence: Failure to challenge yourself results in loss of discretionary privileges
🤝
T - Teamwork Always

Work together with positive attitude. Include everyone - no one left behind. Contribute at family meetings.

Virtues Cultivated: Love (seeking the good of the whole family), Kindness (helping others without expecting reward), Goodness (working for everyone's blessing and success)

Scripture: "Bear one another's burdens, and so fulfill the law of Christ." - Galatians 6:2

Consequence Ladder: Solo timeout → Must complete joint task to rejoin group activities

Part V: Our Restorative Justice Framework

Our home is governed by grace. Consequences are designed to be short, skill-building, and reversible. We do not use shame or yelling. We focus on restorative justice—making things right—administered with love and resolved with speed.

The Repair Principle: When a mistake is made, a 5-minute Repair Window (shown on a timer) begins. A successful repair prevents any further consequence.

The 3 R's: Our Path to Restoration

A mistake happens or harm is caused...

1

Recognize

Acknowledge what went wrong without excuses

2

Repair

Fix the mistake (apologize, clean up, make it right)

3

Restore

Reconnect with the person you wronged and return to peace

Immediate Reset Tools

Gentle Redo: Say or do it again with the correct tone and action.
Pause & Think: A brief cool-down (1–5 minutes) to breathe and pray.
Calm Space: Separate to cool down before using the Peace Code.

Part VI: Governance & Systems

🏛️ The Family Council

Weekly Meeting (Sunday Evening)

We hold a weekly Family Council meeting (usually Sunday evening).

Agenda:
1. 💝 Opening/Appreciation: Share appreciation for a family member (Sharing gratitude for each other)
2. 📅 Logistics: Review the week ahead (Calendar review)
3. 🎉 Celebrations: Acknowledge wins and virtues shown
4. 🔧 Problem-Solving: Discuss challenges together
5. 🎮 Closing: Plan a fun family activity

The Appeal Process

You may make one respectful appeal to Dad with new information. You may also write out a second appeal if you feel like your first appeal did not capture what you had to say. But that is the final appeal.

Part VIII: Dad's Rules

Dad's Sacred Commitment

"Fathers, do not exasperate your children; instead, bring them up in the training and instruction of the Lord." - Ephesians 6:4

Dad is not above the rules. Dad is accountable to the family through these promises. Dad is accountable to the family and this Constitution.

  • 1. Calm Voice Always: No yelling. 60-second reset if needed. Dad will use a 60-second reset if needed.
  • 2. Clear Expectations: Instructions will be clear and given in advance.
  • 3. Daily Connection: Each child receives dedicated one-on-one time. Dedicated one-on-one time with each child daily.
  • 4. Full Listening: No interrupting, reflect back what was heard. Dad will not interrupt.
  • 5. Explain the "Why": Reasons behind rules explained when feasible.
  • 6. Public Accountability: Mistakes require public apology and repair. Dad will apologize publicly and use the 3 R's when he makes mistakes.
  • 7. Predictability: Notice given for schedule changes.
  • 8. Emotional Safety: Model healthy emotional expression. Dad will control his own emotions to model healthy expression.
  • 9. Grace First: Repair always prioritized over punishment. Repair is the priority over punishment.

Our Commitment to How We're Wired (ADHD Accommodations)

We recognize that God made our brains differently, and we adapt to support each other:

  • Visual timers and picture labels are standard practice
  • Tasks broken into manageable chunks. Breaking tasks into chunks.
  • Movement breaks encouraged
  • Extra patience for executive function challenges. Labels are standard practice. We offer extra patience for challenges.
  • Grace without excuses - trusting God's grace to enable the impossible. We will not use ADHD as an excuse but will trust God's grace to enable us to do what is impossible.
