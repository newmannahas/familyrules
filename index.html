Part I: Our Family Mission & Vision

Our Guiding Purpose

Our family's guiding purpose is to create a Christian environment, grounded in the faith maintained by the Body of Christ, the One Holy Catholic and Apostolic Church, the Orthodox Church. This environment is where we are formed in virtue, as we discern the Holy Spirit's specific goals for each of us and are conformed to the likeness of Christ.

Our faith is the center of our home; we pray daily for wisdom and depend upon God's grace in our interactions.

Behold, children are a heritage from the Lord, The fruit of the womb is a reward. - Psalm 127:3
You shall teach them diligently to your children, and shall talk of them when you sit in your house, when you walk by the way, when you lie down, and when you rise up. - Deuteronomy 6:7

Part V: Our Restorative Justice Framework

Our home is governed by grace. We focus on restoration, not punishment.

1

Recognize

Acknowledge what went wrong without excuses

2

Repair

Fix the mistake (apologize, clean up, make it right)

3

Restore

Reconnect with the person you wronged

The Repair Principle

When a mistake is made, a Repair Window begins. This is a 5-minute period, shown on a visible timer, to fix the problem. A successful repair within this window prevents any further consequence.

Part VIII: Our Commitments

üë® Dad's Commitment

Fathers, do not exasperate your children; instead, bring them up in the training and instruction of the Lord. - Ephesians 6:4
  • Calm Voice Always: No yelling. Dad will use a 60-second reset if needed
  • Clear Expectations: Instructions will be clear, concise, and given in advance
  • Daily Connection: Each child receives dedicated one-on-one time each day
  • Full Listening: Dad will not interrupt and will reflect back what he heard
  • Explain the "Why": When feasible, the reasons behind rules will be explained
  • Public Accountability: Dad's mistakes will require a public apology and repair
  • Predictability: Notice will be given for changes to schedule or expectations
  • Emotional Safety: Dad will control his own emotions to model healthy expression
  • Grace First: Repair will always be the priority over punishment

üß† Our Commitment to How We're Wired (ADHD Accommodations)

We recognize that God made our brains differently, and we adapt to support each other. Visual timers, breaking tasks into chunks, movement breaks, and picture labels are standard practice. We offer extra patience for executive function challenges. While we will show grace for each other on account of our ADHD challenges, we will not use this as an excuse but will trust God's grace to enable us to do what is impossible.
