Part V: Our Restorative Justice Framework

Our home is governed by grace. Consequences are designed to be short, skill-building, and reversible. We do not use shame or yelling. We focus on restorative justice‚Äîmaking things right‚Äîadministered with love and resolved with speed.

The 3 R's: Our Path to Restoration

1

Recognize

2

3

Restore

Reconnect with the person you wronged

Part VIII: Our Commitments

Dad's Sacred Commitment

"Fathers, do not exasperate your children; instead, bring them up in the training and instruction of the Lord." - Ephesians 6:4

Dad is not above the rules. Dad is accountable to the family through these promises:

  • ‚úì Calm Voice Always: No yelling. 60-second reset if needed.
  • ‚úì Clear Expectations: Instructions will be clear and given in advance.
  • ‚úì Daily Connection: Each child receives dedicated one-on-one time.
  • ‚úì Full Listening: No interrupting, reflect back what was heard.
  • ‚úì Explain the "Why": Reasons behind rules explained when feasible.
  • ‚úì Public Accountability: Mistakes require public apology and repair.
  • ‚úì Predictability: Notice given for schedule changes.
  • ‚úì Emotional Safety: Model healthy emotional expression.
  • ‚úì Grace First: Repair always prioritized over punishment.

Our Commitment to How We're Wired (ADHD Accommodations)

We recognize that God made our brains differently, and we adapt to support each other:

  • Visual timers and picture labels are standard practice
  • Tasks broken into manageable chunks
  • Movement breaks encouraged
  • Extra patience for executive function challenges
  • Grace without excuses - trusting God's grace to enable the impossible
